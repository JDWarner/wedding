Allow us to reintroduce ourselves

We are pleased to announce that after nearly 7 years together Katy Huff and Matthew Strom Borman are finally getting married!

In September 2007, it all started in the University of Chicago Pub. There, Strom, a first year graduate student, introduced himself to Katy, a fourth year undergrad. She was out with her friends and he with his. When she got up from the booth he intercepted her and said, "I like your bike." Her bike, of course, was parked outside. He must have had his eye on her for a while!

After a year together in Chicago, Katy went to graduate school at the University of Wisconsin in Madison. Strom (and Freddie) drove the UHaul van. Strom returned to Chicago a single man, but we were determined to stay friends. Of course, while we were apart, no one was very happy, so by Valentine's day they were back together, if long distance. They spent a few years riding the bus between Madison and Chicago.

In 2011, Katy moved back to Chicago to finish her dissertation remotely by working with Argonne National Lab. Strom overcame his solitary tendencies and moved in with Katy at Bowers co-op, where we spent two beautiful years.

In 2013, we both got our PhDs and moved to California. Katy is a postdoc at the University of California Berkeley. Strom, however, has a postdoctoral apointment that spans both coasts. His three year postdoc is based at Stanford, but for the 9 months before our wedding, he will live in Princeton while he is a member at the Institute for Advanced Study.

You may have thought Strom would never buy a suit. You may have thought Katy has no time to be a bride! You were not alone in your reasoning. We thought the same things for many years. But now, all that has changed. So, believe it!

We'd like to keep this whole thing very simple, so the guest list will be intimate, the venue will be familiar, and all attire will be welcome.
